My name is Matthew Elia and I am Electrical Engineering major at Northeastern University. To date I have completed my first experiential education experience with a 6 month internship at Philips Color Kinetics, a architectual LED lighting company within Philips Lighting. This was a great experience in which I learned many useful technical skills such as microscope soldering and prototype construction as well as and introduction in PCB design by using the software Altium to design a connector board for a project. Other relevant experience that I have includes work in a research lab run by Prof. Mark Niedre at Northeastern Univerisity know as the Biomedical Optics Research Group. Here I worked under a PHD student to help with with there research into blood analysis through the use of lasers. This was my first hands on engineering experience outside the classroom and I was able to develop some basic skills such as wiring and cabling to better organize their system as well as a brief introduction to lasers and National Instruments DAQ using Matlab and Labview for signal collection.
